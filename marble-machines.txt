My first marble machine
=======================

Ref: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=4fRQgvKlpAE (video)
Ref: http://sangorrin.blogspot.jp/2015/08/my-first-marble-machine.html

List of materials
    - Battery box (AAAx2) with on-off switch: aitendo 90yen
    - Potentiometer with knob (regulate speed): aitendo
    - Stainless steel balls (7.14mm, 9/32inch diameter)
        + 7mm diameter available at Tokyu hands
    - Motor:
        + power with batteries
            Ref: http://www.precisionmicrodrives.com/tech-blog/2013/02/01/tips-for-powering-motors-with-batteries
            - Typically: 2 batteries for 3V
            - runningtime(h) = batterycapacity(mAh)/operatingcurrent(mA)
                --> 32h = 2300mAh/70mA
                --> you can add a battery in parallel to extend the runningtime
        + MOT114A1B (6V 60RPM 12GA, Sengoku 1280yen)
          Ref: http://www.sengoku.co.jp/mod/sgk_cart/detail.php?code=EEHD-0PHM
          [Alt] RA12W0134SY801 (DC3V, 134:1, 100rpm, 1622yen) Ref: http://www.sengoku.co.jp/mod/sgk_cart/detail.php?code=EEHD-0SDY
          [Alt] RA250100 (DC3~6V, 100:1, 70rpm, 1622)
        + Gear for the shaft of the motor with tap (Sengoku)
		+ Rubber cover (Ishikawa neji)
        + supporter
            - M3 spacers: aitendo
    - CAD design considerations
        + shape and dimensions of the motor shaft
        + part to attach the motor
        + make it in parts that can be mounted/unmounted
    - Brass wire (1.0mm diameter)
	- Spring (Muelle, hikibane)
		+ several types (Ishikawa neji and Tokyu hands)
    - Brass wire (shinchuu)
		+ Radio depaato (box shop)
